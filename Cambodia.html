Cambodia: History, Resilience, and Contemporary Reality

Cambodia is a country of profound beauty, stretching from the dense jungles of the north to the white-sand coastlines of the south. However, understanding modern Cambodia requires confronting its deeply tragic recent history. Between 1975 and 1979, the radical communist Khmer Rouge regime, led by Pol Pot, carried out a brutal genocide that resulted in the deaths of an estimated 1.5 to 2 million people—nearly a quarter of the country's population at the time. Intellectuals, doctors, teachers, and anyone deemed an enemy of the agrarian state were systematically targeted, tortured, and executed.

The trauma of the genocide, coupled with subsequent decades of civil instability, fundamentally altered Cambodia’s demographic and social fabric. It effectively wiped out an entire generation of educated professionals, a loss that hampered the nation's infrastructure, education, and economic development for decades. Today, Cambodia is a remarkably young country, with a majority of the population born after the regime's collapse. While the scars remain visible in the lingering poverty, corruption, and the collective memory of its people, the country is defined by an incredible resilience. Visitors will find a population that is exceptionally warm, forward-looking, and eager to share the rich cultural heritage that survived the dark eras of the 20th century.

Mosquito-Borne Diseases & Health Advice

When traveling through Cambodia's tropical landscapes, protecting yourself from mosquitoes is a critical health priority. The region presents risks for several notable vector-borne illnesses:

Dengue Fever

Highly prevalent across Cambodia, including in urban areas like Phnom Penh and Siem Reap. It is transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes, which are most active during the daytime. Symptoms include severe joint and muscle pain, high fever, and rashes.

Malaria

While the risk is low in major cities and tourist centers, malaria remains a threat in more remote, forested, and rural areas, particularly near international borders. It is transmitted by night-biting Anopheles mosquitoes.

Zika & Chikungunya

Both viruses are present in Cambodia and transmitted by daytime mosquitoes. Symptoms generally mirror mild dengue but can carry additional severe health implications (such as birth defects in the case of Zika).

Essential Health Prevention Tactics

  • ✓ Use High-Quality Repellent: Apply repellents containing DEET, Picaridin, or IR3535 consistently throughout the day and evening.
  • ✓ Dress Appropriately: Wear loose-fitting, long-sleeved shirts and long pants, preferably treated with permethrin.
  • ✓ Sleep Safely: Ensure accommodations have secure window screens or use a mosquito net over your bed in rural or open-air settings.
  • ✓ Consult a Travel Clinic: Speak with a doctor before your trip to discuss whether prescription malaria prophylaxis is recommended.

The Ultimate Cambodia Itinerary

01

Phnom Penh — 2 Days

Cambodia's bustling capital is a city of sharp contrasts, where rapid modern development meets historical gravity. Spend your time here understanding the country's past by visiting the Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum (S-21) and the Choeung Ek Genocidal Center. Witnessing these reminders of the Khmer Rouge regime provides vital context for your journey. Balance these heavy visits with a trip to the magnificent Royal Palace and a sunset stroll along the vibrant Sisowath Quay riverside.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Pay your respects at the moving Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum (S-21)
  • ⚡ Visit the historical Choeung Ek Genocidal Center (Killing Fields)
  • ⚡ Admire the grand architecture of the Royal Palace and Silver Pagoda
  • ⚡ Take a scenic sunset walk down the lively Sisowath Quay riverside
02

Kampot and Kep — 4 Days

Head south to experience Cambodia's laid-back side. Kampot is famous for its French colonial architecture and world-renowned pepper plantations, nestled against a backdrop of stunning rural wetlands and mountains. Spend a couple of days exploring the quiet river networks, then head to the nearby coastal town of Kep. Kep is famous for its bustling crab market, fresh seafood, and relaxed coastal vibe—making this 4-day stretch the perfect antidote to the high energy of the capital.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Tour a traditional organic pepper plantation in Kampot
  • ⚡ Rent a kayak or take a sunset cruise along the peaceful Kampot River
  • ⚡ Feast on fresh seafood with green pepper at the famous Kep Crab Market
  • ⚡ Explore the crumbling relics of French colonial villas throughout Kep
03

Koh Rong Islands — 1 to 2 Weeks

For pristine white sands and turquoise waters, the Koh Rong islands are paradise personified. You will need to take a ferry or speed boat from the coastal city of Sihanoukville to reach the islands.

⚠️ Travel Safety Note: Sihanoukville has undergone intense, rapid development and can feel chaotic and unsafe for solo or unwary travelers. The boat transfers can also vary in safety standards depending on weather conditions. Because of this, it is highly recommended to spend at most 1 night in Sihanoukville to align with your ferry schedule, getting you out to the islands as quickly and smoothly as possible. Once on the islands, you can easily unwind for a week or two.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Unwind on the blindingly white sands of Saracen Bay or Long Beach
  • ⚡ Swim with glowing bioluminescent plankton on a nighttime boat tour
  • ⚡ Snorkel or scuba dive in the clear tropical reefs surrounding the islands
  • ⚡ Hike through island jungle paths connecting secluded coastal bays
04

Battambang — 2 Days

Battambang is Cambodia's leading artistic hub, offering a blend of culture and unique countryside experiences. Take a ride on the famous local Bamboo Train, explore the surrounding rice paddies, and visit Phnom Sampeau. At dusk, gather at the base of the mountain to witness the incredible spectacle of millions of bats emerging from the caves to hunt.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Ride the legendary local open-air Bamboo Train (Norry)
  • ⚡ Watch millions of bats pour out of the Phnom Sampeau caves at dusk
  • ⚡ Explore the historic French-colonial buildings in the town center
  • ⚡ Check out the vibrant galleries and contemporary local art scenes
05

Siem Reap — 4 Days

No trip to Cambodia is complete without visiting Siem Reap, the gateway to the ancient Khmer Empire. Spend four days exploring the sprawling Angkor Archaeological Park. You will discover iconic structures like Ta Prohm, where massive tree roots have grown directly into the stone masonry, alongside the majestic, towering stone structures of the main temple complexes. Be sure to pace yourself, catch an Angkor Wat sunrise, and explore the town's vibrant night markets and dining scene.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Watch the iconic sunrise over the towers of Angkor Wat
  • ⚡ See the massive tree roots overtaking the stone at Ta Prohm
  • ⚡ Marvel at the giant, serene stone faces carved into Bayon Temple
  • ⚡ Experience the energetic street food stalls and markets along Pub Street
