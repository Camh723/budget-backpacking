The Philippines: Clear Lagoons & World-Class Diving

With over 7,600 islands featuring some of the clearest turquoise waters on earth, navigating the vast geography of the Philippines is a truly epic adventure. Because these tropical gems are separated by deep marine channels and expansive seas, traveling between them relies heavily on domestic flights and seasonal ferry routes. This makes it absolutely essential to book your flights and key boat transfers in advance to avoid getting stranded or paying exorbitant last-minute prices.

Beyond the powdery white-sand shorelines and dramatic limestone walls, the Philippines is a legendary mecca for underwater enthusiasts. This blueprint is specifically designed as an action-packed 3 to 4-week route crossing 5 to 6 of the country's most spectacular islands. From swimming with massive sardine runs and searching for thresher sharks to diving historic WWII shipwrecks, this island-hopping trail guarantees an unforgettable look at the most spectacular marine life in Southeast Asia.

Daily Budget ~$30 - $50 USD

Backpacker Flight Operations, Island Time & Diving Standards

Unlike overland trails in continental Southeast Asia, backpacking the Philippines requires dynamic planning. Navigating ocean swell, remote airport layouts, and marine parks demands some crucial insider tips:

Pre-Book Domestic Flights

To cover islands like Siargao, Cebu, and Palawan within a month, you must rely on domestic budget airlines (primarily Cebu Pacific and AirAsia). Flight prices surge drastically and regional planes sell out weeks in advance. Lock in these key transit legs early to protect your itinerary.

World-Class Diving Hubs

The Philippines sits directly in the Coral Triangle, boasting unmatched marine biodiversity. Whether you are getting your PADI certification, exploring deep historical WWII shipwrecks in Coron, or swimming alongside rare thresher sharks in Malapascua, the underwater scenes here are legendary.

Weather & Island Time

Everything in the archipelago runs on "island time." Due to typhoons or heavy monsoonal winds, ferry lines and flights are regularly delayed or cancelled entirely. Always build a "buffer day" into your travel schedule before an international flight back home out of Manila.

Essential Pack Track Tactics

  • ✓ Cash Backup Required: ATMs on islands like El Nido and Siquijor frequently run out of physical cash or suffer power outages. Keep a reserve of Pesos.
  • ✓ Keep Terminal Cash: Be prepared to pay small, local environmental fees and terminal taxes in cash at almost every ferry port and domestic airport.
  • ✓ Invest in a Dry Bag: Island-hopping tours in traditional outrigger boats (bangkas) involve splashing seawater. A waterproof dry bag is mandatory gear.
  • ✓ Skip Manila Traffic: Manila is heavily congested and complex to navigate. Try to transit through its terminals and fly out to the islands as fast as possible.

The Ultimate 4-Week Island Route

01

Manila — 1 Day

Your journey begins in the high-intensity capital of Manila on the main island of Luzon. As the major international aviation gateway, you will almost certainly land here. While many backpackers choose to head straight to the domestic terminal to catch flights out to the islands, spending one day lets you adjust to the timezone. If you do stay, explore the historic Spanish colonial walled city of Intramuros and visit Fort Santiago to gain a deep appreciation for the unique historical fusion of Spanish, American, and Filipino cultures.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Walk through the cobblestone streets of the historic Intramuros walled city
  • ⚡ Visit Fort Santiago to learn about national hero José Rizal's history
  • ⚡ Tour the beautiful baroque architecture of San Agustin Church
  • ⚡ Catch a legendary sunset over the ocean horizon at Manila Baywalk
02

Siargao Island — 5 Days

Fly southeast to Siargao, the laid-back surf capital of the Philippines. Tucked away on the edge of the Pacific, this teardrop-shaped island has captured the hearts of backpackers worldwide with its dense palm-tree lined roads, amazing health-food cafes, and vibrant social culture. Spend five days surfing the legendary reef breaks of Cloud 9, renting a scooter to explore the endless coconut plantations of the interior, and taking a day boat out to the neighboring paradisiacal islets of Guyam, Daku, and Naked Island.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Surf or watch pros on the iconic wooden boardwalk at Cloud 9 break
  • ⚡ Island hop on a bangka to Naked Island, Daku Island, and Guyam Island
  • ⚡ Rent a scooter to ride through the sea of palms at the Coconut Lookout
  • ⚡ Rope swing and cliff jump into the pristine waters of Maasin River
03

Bohol — 4 Days

Fly into Cebu and take a fast ferry over to Bohol, a diverse island packed with spectacular inland geological wonders and unique wildlife. Over four days, you can rent a scooter to witness the iconic Chocolate Hills—over a thousand symmetrical cone-shaped hills that turn chocolate-brown during the dry season. Stop by a sanctuary to see the tiny, endangered tarsiers (one of the world's smallest primates), cruise down the emerald waters of the Loboc River, and cross over the bridge to Panglao Island for gorgeous sandy beaches and great shore-snorkeling spots.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Marvel at the endless geological mounds from the Chocolate Hills viewpoint
  • ⚡ See the tiny, nocturnal primates at the Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary
  • ⚡ Eat lunch on a floating river boat cruise along the lush Loboc River
  • ⚡ Relax on Alona Beach and snorkel the marine sanctuaries of Panglao
04

Siquijor — 4 Days

Take a direct ferry from Bohol to Siquijor, a mystical island historically famous for its local legends of witchcraft, healers, and magic. Today, Siquijor is an absolute favorite for backpackers seeking laid-back coastal roads and natural wonders. Spend four days swing-diving from ropes into the tiered turquoise pools of Cambugahay Falls, cliff-jumping into the ocean at Salagdoong Beach, and exploring underwater marine sanctuaries directly from the beach.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Swing from Tarzan ropes into the milky-blue pools of Cambugahay Falls
  • ⚡ Leap off concrete diving platforms into the sea at Salagdoong Beach
  • ⚡ Visit the mystical, 400-year-old Old Enchanted Balete Tree and fish spa
  • ⚡ Ride a scooter completely around the island's scenic perimeter highway
🤿 World-Class Day Trip: Apo Island Siquijor is the absolute best base to arrange a diving or snorkeling day trip out to Apo Island. This protected volcanic marine reserve is home to some of the most pristine coral gardens and sea turtle populations in Asia.
05

Cebu (Moalboal) — 4 Days

Ferry back to Cebu Island and travel south down the coast to the adventure hub of Moalboal. This coastal town offers two mind-blowing backpacker highlights. First, you can swim just 30 meters off Panagsama Beach to witness the legendary Sardine Run—snorkeling directly over millions of swirling sardines that block out the sun in massive, moving bait balls. Second, take a short day trip to Badian to experience canyoneering down the cliffs and jungle ravines of the famous, multi-tiered Kawasan Falls.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Snorkel with the massive, swirling Sardine Run right off Panagsama Beach
  • ⚡ Trek, slide, and cliff-jump through canyons to reach Kawasan Falls
  • ⚡ Spot giant sea turtles grazing on sea grass fields close to the shore
  • ⚡ Rent a scooter and head into the hills to climb rugged Osmeña Peak
06

Palawan (El Nido & Coron) — 1 Week

Conclude your journey by flying from Cebu over to the world-renowned province of Palawan. Split your week between El Nido and Coron. In El Nido, take iconic boat tours past towering limestone monoliths to kayak in the crystal-clear waters of the Big Lagoon and Secret Beach. Next, take a ferry or a multi-day expedition boat over to Coron. Coron is famous for holding some of the most remarkable scuba diving settings in the world, featuring a fleet of massive, preserved WWII Japanese supply shipwrecks lying at recreational diving depths.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Kayak between giant karst formations in El Nido's emerald Big Lagoon
  • ⚡ Dive historical, coral-encrusted WWII Japanese shipwrecks in Coron
  • ⚡ Swim in the hyper-clear, mixed salt and fresh waters of Kayangan Lake
  • ⚡ Watch an incredible tropical sunset over the shores of Nacpan Beach
