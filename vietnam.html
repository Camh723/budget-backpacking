Vietnam: The Dragon Trail of Southeast Asia

Vietnam is an epic, vertical odyssey. Stretching from the towering, razor-sharp karst ridges of the extreme north down to the endless, maze-like waterways of the Mekong Delta, this country demands momentum. It is a land built for transit—whether you are overnighting on sleeper trains, navigating legendary mountain passes on two wheels, or weaving through neon-lit, scooter-packed colonial alleys. From mist-shrouded rice terraces to lantern-lit heritage ports, Vietnam offers an intense, sensory-rich backpacking track that stands out as one of the most diverse on Earth.

Daily Budget ~$30 - $40 USD
Local Currency Vietnamese Dong (VND)
Primary Transit Sleeper Bus / Motorbike
Best Food Hack Bánh Mì & Cà Phê Trứng

⏱️ Note on Timeframes: The day allocations listed across both regions are based on my personal track. Think of them as a highly recommended pacing blueprint, but remember—backpacking is entirely fluid. Spend as little or as long as you want in each spot; stay where you vibe, and pull the pin when you're ready to chase the next horizon.

Part I: Northern Vietnam — Chaos, Canyons & Mystical Bays

The North contains the country's most dramatic landscapes. It is home to old colonial cities, world-famous emerald bays, and jaw-dropping mountain loops that border China.

01

Hanoi — 3 to 4 Days

Hanoi is a striking mix of French-colonial charm and absolute scooter chaos. The heartbeat of the city is found in the narrow alleys of the Old Quarter, where motorbikes weave around outdoor Bia Hơi stalls and street food vendors. Spend your days drinking rich egg coffee, watching trains brush past buildings on Train Street, and soaking in the evening energy around Hoan Kiem Lake. Hanoi is also your logistical command center—use this time to research, choose, and book your upcoming Ha Giang Loop expedition directly from here.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Explore the bustling alleys of the Old Quarter
  • ⚡ Sip an authentic hot Egg Coffee (Cà Phê Trứng)
  • ⚡ Catch a train passing close by on the famous Train Street
  • ⚡ Book your all-inclusive Ha Giang Loop transit package
02

Ha Giang Loop — 4 Days (Book from Hanoi)

The Ha Giang Loop is an unforgettable, legendary road trip. Over four days, you will ride through massive mountain passes, navigate deep canyons, and look out over vertical limestone peaks right on the edge of the Chinese border. Whether you choose to ride solo or book an "easy rider" guide to steer for you, the sheer scale of the landscape is mind-blowing. The evenings are spent in small ethnic homestays, drinking "happy water" and sharing stories with fellow travelers.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Ride along the edge of the deep Ma Pi Leng Pass
  • ⚡ Stay at local minority homestays in Du Gia and Dong Van
  • ⚡ Navigate the iconic, winding switchbacks of Heaven Gate
  • ⚡ Take a longtail boat tour down the Nho Que River gorge
03

Ha Long Bay — 2 Days

Ha Long Bay is a world-renowned natural wonder. It features thousands of limestone pillars rising out of calm, green waters. Spending two days on an overnight boat cruise allows you to sail past floating fishing villages, kayak into hidden sea caves, and watch the sun set over the iconic karst skyline. It's a classic, relaxing experience that lets you unwind after the rugged demands of the Ha Giang Loop.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Cruise on a traditional junk boat through the bay
  • ⚡ Kayak through hidden lagoons and limestone arches
  • ⚡ Explore the massive stalactites inside Sung Sot Cave
  • ⚡ Watch the sunrise with early morning Tai Chi on deck
04

Sapa — 2 Days

Sapa is tucked away in the Hoang Lien Son mountain range, home to Vietnam's highest peak, Fansipan. This highland region is famous for its steep, cascading green rice terraces and rich indigenous culture. Spending two days trekking here gives you the chance to walk through mountain mist, learn about local hill-tribe traditions, and stay in rural villages surrounded by beautiful mountain vistas.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Trek through the green Muong Hoa Valley terraces
  • ⚡ Take the cable car up to the peak of Mount Fansipan
  • ⚡ Stay in an authentic Black Hmong village homestay
  • ⚡ Hike down to the scenic pools of Cat Cat Waterfall
05

Ninh Binh & Tam Coc — 4 Days

Ninh Binh is often called "Ha Long Bay on Land," and it's easy to see why. The landscape features dramatic limestone peaks rising directly out of bright green rice paddies and winding river networks. Setting up base in the laid-back village of Tam Coc for four days allows you to explore the area at your own pace—cycling along rural paths, taking a wooden rowboat through low river caves, and climbing the steep steps of Mua Cave for a fantastic view over the valley below.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Take a rowboat tour through the Trang An or Tam Coc caves
  • ⚡ Climb the 500 stone steps to the top of Hang Mua Peak
  • ⚡ Cycle past rural villages and temples on a rented bike
  • ⚡ Explore the ancient citadel grounds of Hoa Lu

Part II: Central Vietnam — Caves, Imperial Cities & Coastal Passes

Heading south brings you into Vietnam's rich cultural heart. This region is home to massive underground cave systems, ancient imperial cities, and scenic coastal mountain passes.

06

Phong Nha — 2 to 3 Days

Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park is a geological wonderland, housing some of the largest and oldest limestone cave systems on the planet. Spending two to three days here allows you to dive deep into the underground world. You can explore massive caverns lit up by colorful lights, zoom into zip-line mud caves, or trek through wild jungle landscapes. The village itself is small and peaceful, surrounded by green hills and clear rivers.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Marvel at the scale of Paradise Cave and Phong Nha Cave
  • ⚡ Zip-line and swim in the mud inside Dark Cave (Hang Toi)
  • ⚡ Rent a bicycle to explore the peaceful Bong Lai Valley
  • ⚡ Trek out into the protected national park jungle trails
07

Hue — 2 Days

Hue is Vietnam's former imperial capital, full of history and royal heritage. Spend two days exploring the walled Imperial City, visiting royal tombs along the Perfume River, and exploring abandoned water parks. Hue also marks the start of an iconic travel experience—from here, you can rent a motorbike or join an easy rider tour to ride over the famous Hai Van Pass down to Hoi An.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Walk through the grand grounds of the Imperial Citadel
  • ⚡ Explore the eerie, overgrown Abandoned Water Park
  • ⚡ Ride a motorbike across the scenic Hai Van Pass
  • ⚡ Visit the grand royal tomb of Emperor Khai Dinh
08

Hoi An — 4 Days

Hoi An is a beautiful, lantern-lit town that feels frozen in time. The yellow-walled Ancient Town is a pedestrian-only zone, lined with historic merchant houses, tailor shops, and cozy cafes along the river. Spending four days here gives you plenty of time to relax—whether you want to get custom clothes made, cycle down to nearby beaches, or take a basket boat ride through mangrove forests. In the evening, the town lights up with hundreds of colorful lanterns, making it one of the most scenic spots on the entire trail.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Walk the yellow-walled streets of the Ancient Town
  • ⚡ Get custom-tailored clothes made at a local shop
  • ⚡ Ride a traditional bamboo basket boat in Cam Thanh
  • ⚡ Release a small paper lantern onto the river at night

Part III: Southern Vietnam — Highlands, Dunes & Energy Hubs

The southern leg shifts into rolling highland hills, coastal desert sand dunes, and vibrant, fast-paced metropolises.

09

Dalat — 3 to 4 Days

Dalat is nestled in the Central Highlands, offering a cool mountain escape from the coastal heat. This alpine town is famous for its pine forests, lakes, and French-style villas. Over three to four days, you can dive into outdoor adventures like canyoning down waterfalls, explore the surreal architecture of the Crazy House, and enjoy the thriving local cafe scene. It's a unique stop that feels completely different from the rest of the country.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Go canyoning and abseiling down Datanla Waterfalls
  • ⚡ Explore the strange, artistic rooms of the Crazy House
  • ⚡ Drink local highland coffee overlooking pine valleys
  • ⚡ Ride the alpine coaster through the forest trails
10

Mui Ne — 2 Days (Optional Extra)

Mui Ne is a unique coastal town famous for its massive desert sand dunes. Spending two days here allows you to see a totally different side of Vietnam's geography. You can rent a quad bike to zoom over the white dunes, slide down the red dunes at sunset, and walk through the shallow waters of the Fairy Stream flanked by strange clay formations. It's an optional, fast-paced desert stop right along the ocean.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Watch the sunset over the Red Sand Dunes
  • ⚡ Ride an off-road ATV across the White Sand Dunes
  • ⚡ Walk barefoot through the shallow Fairy Stream canyon
  • ⚡ Visit the local fishing harbor packed with round boats
11

Saigon — 3 Days

Saigon (Ho Chi Minh City) is a high-energy metropolis where modern skyscrapers tower over historic temples and street markets. Over three days, you can dive deep into the country's wartime history at the War Remnants Museum, explore the massive underground Cu Chi Tunnels network, and experience the nightlife on Bui Vien Street. Saigon marks the final chapter of your Vietnamese journey—from this southern hub, you can easily organize a bus connection or passport stamps to cross the border overland straight into Cambodia.

Essential Things To Do

  • ⚡ Visit the moving exhibits at the War Remnants Museum
  • ⚡ Crawl through the historic underground Cu Chi Tunnels
  • ⚡ Dive into the chaotic night market at Ben Thanh
  • ⚡ Secure your overland bus ticket connection into Cambodia
